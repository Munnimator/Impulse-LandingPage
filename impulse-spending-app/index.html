An impulse spending app built for the moment you are about to buy.

ImpulseLog is not a full budgeting suite. It is a focused iPhone app for logging resisted purchases, slowing down with a wait timer, tracking mood and context, and turning “I almost bought that” into visible savings momentum.

Why this page exists

What this does that a blog post does not

A blog post helps you understand the problem

It explains emotional spending, ADHD patterns, or strategies that can help. That is useful, but it often leaves the next action open-ended.

This page shows the product workflow

It ties one search intent to one job: open the app, log the urge, pause the purchase, and build momentum toward a savings goal.

How ImpulseLog works

A tighter path from urge to saved money

1. Catch the impulse

Instead of switching between shopping tabs and mental math, you log the item, amount, and context in one place.

2. Put the purchase on pause

Use the wait timer when you want friction before buying, especially for items that feel urgent in the moment.

3. See progress immediately

Resisted purchases feed your Impulse Ledger, savings goals, streaks, and challenges so self-control feels visible.

Inside the app

What the workflow looks like on screen

Dashboard and streak momentum

The dashboard keeps your recent wins, goals, and daily challenges visible so impulse control feels like progress, not deprivation.

Analytics that show the pattern

Review what you resisted, where the money would have gone, and which categories or moments create the most friction.
